# Creative Reference Taxonomy

This taxonomy turns references into reusable design evidence without turning
famous products into style authority. It extends the Reference Quality Ladder
with curated local packs that agents can use before optional web research.

## Path Selection

Fast path applies when an approved design system already covers the target and
the user asks for a routine screen, small refinement, or narrow extension. Use
approved tokens and components first; do not run full creative research.

Medium path applies when a new feature needs a fresh screen pattern, stronger
interaction treatment, or a small campaign/landing section while the visual
system remains stable. Use one or two curated packs and record borrow/avoid.

Full creative path applies to new products, rebrands, new visual direction,
major landing pages, or category repositioning. Generate two or three distinct
directions, each grounded in different packs, local evidence, and reference
roles.

## Reference Role

- `creative benchmark`: craft, atmosphere, narrative, composition, or material
  language worth translating into a new context.
- `interaction benchmark`: a specific flow or behavior pattern worth adapting.
- `category convention`: what users already expect from the market.
- `direct competitor`: parity, risk, and differentiation evidence.
- `platform standard`: official behavior and accessibility constraints.
- `implementation library`: engineering adapter constraints, never taste
  authority.
- `anti-pattern`: a failure mode to avoid.
- `do-not-use-as-style`: an undecomposed brand analogy that must be broken into
  explicit borrow and avoid traits.

## Quality Tier

- `tier-1`: current, captured, directly relevant, role-labeled, and cited with
  borrow, avoid, and fit rationale.
- `tier-2`: local curated pack, official platform guidance, adjacent category
  evidence, or local intelligence row with clear constraints.
- `tier-3`: old, partial, undated, or historical material. Use only as context
  or anti-pattern evidence.

## Pack Selection

Select packs by product pressure, not by taste preference:

- Dense operational surfaces: data products, regulated trust, mobile native
  when target is mobile.
- New brand or landing: editorial, luxury, experimental web, AI products, or
  regulated trust based on audience risk.
- Developer tools: devtools without product mimicry, data products, AI products,
  and interaction benchmarks from the actual workflow.
- Consumer or creator products: mobile native, editorial, experimental web, and
  luxury when restraint or craft is the differentiator.

## Golden Briefs

Use these briefs as internal evals for design agents:

- devtool landing: must avoid famous-product style authority and cite the
  devtools pack plus one non-SaaS creative benchmark.
- fintech dashboard: must combine regulated trust with data products and name
  what visual novelty is accessibility-safe.
- AI coding app: must combine AI products, devtools, and interaction benchmark
  references without copying chat shells blindly.
- healthcare onboarding: must prioritize regulated trust, plain language, and
  calm interaction states.
- creator mobile app: must use mobile-native and editorial packs while proving
  touch ergonomics.
- dense admin console: must keep fast scanning and data density while adding
  one ownable brand move.

## Output Contract

Every chosen pack contributes:

- pack id and path;
- reference role and quality tier;
- why this pack fits the product;
- what to borrow;
- what to avoid;
- differentiation pressure;
- whether the run is fast path, medium path, or full creative path.
